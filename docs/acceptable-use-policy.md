## 1. Who this applies to

This AUP applies to every organization that applies for or receives Free For
Charity (FFC) services, and to the people who act on the organization's behalf —
the applicant, board members, staff, and volunteers who are given access to any
FFC-provided or FFC-managed account, domain, mailbox, repository, or tool.

## 2. What FFC provides (and that it is free)

FFC provides, at no cost, an integrated stack for eligible nonprofits — typically
a domain, professional email, a website you can maintain yourself, design assets,
analytics, access to donated software, and trained volunteer support. FFC is a
volunteer-run 501(c)(3); services are provided **as-is**, without warranty, and
may change or be discontinued.

## 3. The full-stack commitment (core term)

FFC delivers an **integrated stack**, not an à-la-carte menu. By accepting this
policy the organization agrees to **adopt and use the complete Free For Charity
stack as designed** — not to pick and choose individual pieces while declining
the rest. This includes, as applicable to the organization:

- The FFC-managed **domain** and **DNS**.
- **Professional email** on the FFC-provisioned platform (Microsoft 365 or, where
  applicable, Google Workspace) on the organization's domain.
- The **website** FFC builds, maintained through the standard FFC workflow
  (GitHub-based, AI-assisted editing).
- The **security baseline**: multi-factor authentication on every account,
  a holistic password-management approach, and accounts that represent **real
  people, not shared anonymous logins** (see the setup guides).
- The **supporting accounts** the program depends on (e.g. Candid/GuideStar,
  TechSoup, the organization's social presence).

Why this matters: the stack is secure, recoverable, and maintainable **because**
the pieces work together. Partial adoption creates security gaps and support
burden that undermine the free model for every charity FFC serves. An
organization that does not intend to use the full stack is not a fit for FFC.

## 4. Eligibility & prerequisites

Acceptance of this policy does not by itself grant service. The organization must
also meet the eligibility floor and complete the prerequisite steps documented in
the [application & prerequisites inventory](./application-prerequisites-inventory.md),
including the smaller-charity revenue gate, the personal/organizational account
setup, and the validation checks.

## 5. Account & security responsibilities

Each person with access agrees to:

- Use their **own** account in their **real name**, secured with
  **multi-factor authentication**, and **never share credentials** except through
  approved credential-sharing tooling at the organizational phase.
- Use a **unique password for every service** and never reuse passwords —
  protecting **email and banking** above all (see the password-manager guide).
- Keep **MFA recovery codes** stored safely so a lost device is never a lockout.
- Use only the **approved, supported browsers** (Chrome or Edge) for FFC work.
- Promptly report suspected account compromise to FFC.

## 6. Acceptable use

FFC accounts, domains, sites, and tools may be used only for the organization's
**lawful, charitable purpose**. Prohibited uses include, without limitation:

- Illegal activity, fraud, or misrepresentation of the organization or its status.
- Sending spam or operating phishing/malware; infringing intellectual property.
- Sharing access with, or transferring assets to, unrelated third parties.
- Using FFC resources for partisan political campaigning or for private benefit
  inconsistent with 501(c)(3) status.
- Attempting to circumvent the security baseline in Section 5.

## 7. Data handling & privacy

The organization is responsible for the data it stores in FFC-provided systems
and agrees to handle constituent and donor data lawfully and respectfully —
collecting only what is needed, restricting access to those who need it, and
honoring applicable privacy laws (U.S. state privacy laws first, then GDPR where
relevant), consistent with FFC's own
[privacy policy](https://ffcadmin.org/privacy-policy/). The organization will not
upload data it has no right to hold.

## 8. Conflict of interest

People acting for the organization must disclose any conflict of interest (for
example, a financial interest in a vendor or a personal relationship that could
improperly influence decisions about FFC-provided resources) and must not use
their access for personal gain. Board members accept the responsibility and
liability of their role, which they signal publicly by linking the charity under
their LinkedIn "Volunteering" section.

## 9. Branding & attribution

The organization agrees to the **"credit FFC"** expectation — keeping the
standard, unobtrusive Free For Charity attribution that FFC includes with
delivered work, unless FFC agrees otherwise in writing.

## 10. Changes, offboarding & termination

FFC may update this policy; material changes will be communicated to active
organizations. An organization may leave at any time; FFC will support a
reasonable offboarding (handing over the domain and content the charity owns).
FFC may suspend or end service for violations of this policy, loss of eligibility,
or abandonment of the prerequisites or the full-stack commitment.

## 11. Acknowledgment

Acceptance is recorded when an authorized representative of the organization
acknowledges this policy through the FFC application issue-template form. That
acknowledgment confirms the organization has read this AUP and agrees to the
**full-stack commitment** (Section 3), the **security responsibilities**
(Section 5), the **conflict-of-interest & data-handling** terms (Sections 7–8),
and the **attribution** expectation (Section 9).
