# Charity Onboarding Acknowledgment

> The short acknowledgment a charity confirms during the FFC application (captured
> via the application issue-template form). The binding terms live in the
> **[Terms of Service](https://ffcadmin.org/terms-of-service/)** — Section 18
> (Supported Charities). This page is the plain-language summary the applicant
> agrees to; the Terms of Service are the controlling version.

By applying for Free For Charity services, an authorized representative of the
organization confirms they are **authorized to commit the organization** and that
the organization agrees to:

- **Use the whole FFC stack as designed** — domain, email, website, security
  baseline, design, analytics, and supporting accounts — not pick-and-choose.
- **Run real, secured accounts** — every person uses their own account, in their
  real name, with multi-factor authentication; no shared logins.
- **Use it for our lawful, on-mission charitable purpose**, and keep what we
  publish honest, decent, and accessible.
- **Own and protect our data** — ours stays ours; we handle constituent and donor
  data responsibly.
- **Use AI responsibly** — review AI output before publishing; don't paste
  sensitive data where it doesn't belong.
- **Understand what's free vs. paid** — the core stack is free; optional paid
  add-ons are our own cost.
- **Keep our people and access current**, disclose conflicts of interest, and
  **credit FFC**.
- **Understand support is best-effort** (no guaranteed response time) and that
  **continued service depends on staying eligible and current**.

By acknowledging, the organization also agrees to the full
**[Free For Charity Terms of Service](https://ffcadmin.org/terms-of-service/)**,
which is the controlling agreement.
